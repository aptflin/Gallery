畫家序

商場並不是我生命中唯一興趣，舉凡八大藝術——戲劇、文學、電影、音樂、美術、建築、閱讀、甚至魔術戲法或美食等等，一切有情有味「美」的事物，都讓我的生活中多了幾分樂趣與滋味。中外人士嗜用「雅興」妝點這種興致，而令我心馳神往的是藝術中赤誠的生命能量。自幼喜愛繪畫，差點念了美術系，爾後雖念了外交系、經商，工作雖忙碌，卻未曾澆滅對畫畫和賞畫的熱衷，約於2014年開始嘗試藝術平面創作這件事，這項藝術讓我的人格特質——喜愛觀察、不因循蹈軌、熱愛自由和獨立思考得以發揮，透過構圖線條和色彩，揮灑自我對生命狂熾的熱忱。創作至今10年，累積了上千張作品，但追求創新創意的意志和理念從未改變，堅信藝術創作道路上——「要最好，你非變不可」！

繪畫的創作領域從傳統水墨、書法、水彩、壓克力畫等等，各式主題都吸引我嘗試的慾望，喜愛探索材質和挑戰新境，呈現自我風格。從不認為傳統藝術或主題就是守舊，好作品不分傳統或當代，重點是創作者如何透過畫筆詮釋「意境」，因此創作中少不了帶有文化或文學背景的作品，如「五福臨門」、「采菊東籬」、「柿柿如意」或描述親情的畫作，甚至帶有童趣的主題也是畫筆下的常客。疫情期間全球緊張的氛圍和不同的材質提供另類創作思維，諸如「男人男人」、「女人女人」；而好奇世界風情，各國人物文化描述也是創作重點之一；「佛」境的美好，並非創作的點綴，而是核心，禪意寄予一花一草、山川大海、萬物生靈，乃至綠焰之書或孤城萬仞，一切恩典盡在觀自在的慈悲凝視中化為得心自在。

在《In Solitude 有無相生•蘇一仲藝術創作展》即將開幕之際，將這個展獻給摯愛的家人及朋友們，你們是我最重要的珍寶!感謝和泰興業大家庭和全體扶輪社社友對我的愛護，以及社會大眾的支持；謝謝海內外美好人生協會及好友們對我的鼓勵與關心 ! 最後，無論舊識或素未謀面，希望你∕妳因這個展認識我的作品，走進Antonio的繪畫世界，與我分享藝術創作的喜悅，也透過藝術人文表達我對社會大眾的關懷與祝福。

The business world has never been my sole interest in life. The eight arts—drama, literature, film, music, fine arts, architecture, reading, and even the craft of magic or the culinary arts—anything imbued with beauty and vitality has always held a place in my daily life. While many describe this as a cultivated “refined interest,” what captivates me most is the sincere and pure energy of life that art embodies. Since childhood, I have been deeply drawn to painting, and at one point nearly pursued formal studies in fine arts. Afterwards, although I studied in the diplomacy department, and my career later led me into the business world, where responsibilities are often demanding, my passion for painting and art appreciation has never waned. Around 2014, I began to engage with painting more rigorously. Art has become a medium through which my own nature—keen observation, resistance to conformity, love of freedom, and independent thinking, finds expression. In lines and colors, I release my fervent passion for life. Over the past decade, I have created more than a thousand works. Yet my pursuit of innovation and creativity has led me to firmly believe that on the path of artistic creation—“To reach excellence, you must embrace change”. My creative journey spans diverse mediums—traditional Chinese ink painting, calligraphy, watercolor, acrylics, and more—and an endless array of themes constantly ignite my desire to experiment. I love exploring new materials and challenging unfamiliar terrains, shaping a style that is uniquely my own. I never regard traditional art or subject matter as synonymous with conservatism; great works transcend the divide between tradition and contemporary. What truly matters is how the artist interprets the “artistic conception” through the brush. Thus, many of my works naturally draw from cultural and literary inspirations: The Arrival of Auspice, Poem for Chrysanthemums, Harmony in All Things, or pieces that depict the intimacy of family bonds. Playful, childlike themes also frequently appear under my brush. During the pandemic, the tense global atmosphere, combined with my experiments in materials, inspired alternative modes of creation—such as The Men Within and She, in Many Faces. My curiosity about world cultures has also made depictions of people and traditions from different countries one of my central themes. The beauty of the Buddhist realm is not a mere ornament in my art but its very core. Zen finds expression in a single flower or blade of grass, in mountains and seas, in all living beings—even in Scripture of the Verdant Blaze or The Lone Citadel, Ten Thousand Fathoms High. All blessings and grace converge in the compassionate gaze of Bodhisattva, transforming into a freedom and ease of the heart. On the occasion of the forthcoming opening ceremony of “The Interplay of Being and Non-Being — The Art Exhibition of Antonio Soo”, I dedicate this exhibition to my beloved family and friends, who remain my most cherished treasure.
